Aurore Sourdough

Artisan Services

From private rituals to wholesale rhythm planning.

Section One

A service menu with four different moods.

01. Dawn Table Ritual

For private suppers and chef collaborations.

We arrive with just-baked loaves, cultured butter, smoked salts, and a paced serving ritual that opens the meal like a prologue.

02. Neighborhood Supply

For cafes, grocers, and tasting counters.

A cadence-based supply plan built around footfall, weather, and service windows so your shelves never feel guessed at.

03. Starter Stewardship

For teams learning live fermentation.

A workshop built around touch, scent, and timing rather than recipe cards, so the room learns to read dough with confidence.

04. Bread for Launch Nights

For brands that need a warm focal point.

We design a service moment around scent, tearing, plating, and pacing so guests remember the bread as part of the reveal.

Section Two

How an Aurore service day unfolds.

This sequence is designed like choreography rather than fulfillment: every checkpoint is timed to smell, temperature, and guest attention.

1

Calibrate the room

We map guest flow, holding points, plating surfaces, and where the first aroma should land.

2

Build the bake rhythm

Proofing, loading, scoring, and resting are timed backwards from service so the crumb opens at the right minute.

3

Stage the reveal

Our team sets the first slice, service language, garnish cadence, and warm handoff so the room feels intentional.

4

Leave a living system behind

When needed, we leave service notes, holding guides, and starter care plans so the rhythm can continue without us.

Section Three

Choose a format that fits the room.

Each format is intentionally different in scale and tempo, but all four keep the bakery language tactile, warm, and performance-driven.

Salon Table

8 to 18 guests

Small-format dinners with guided bread service, two butter expressions, and plated finishing salts.

Cafe Rhythm

Daily or weekly

Supply planning with product mix, waste controls, and reserve-bake logic for morning traffic spikes.

Workshop Block

2 to 3 hours

A tactile class for teams who want starter confidence, shaping rhythm, and a stronger sensory vocabulary.

Launch Night

40 to 120 guests

An editorial service setup with live slicing, bread carts, warm plating, and guest-facing storytelling.

Section Four

Tell us what kind of room you are serving.

We shape the service around your guest count, timeline, tone, and whether the bread should feel like a supporting element or the headline itself.
