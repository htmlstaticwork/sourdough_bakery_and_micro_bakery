Our Story

Born of flour, fire, and the art of patience.

Crafting a
Living Tradition

I began Aurore Sourdough in 2018 with nothing but stone-milled flour, local spring water, and a jar that would eventually become Maurice, our sourdough starter.

Before starting this bakery, I spent years studying fermentation in the rural bakeries of France. My goal was simple: to bring the honest, nutritive power of slow-fermented bread back to our neighborhood tables.

The Baking Journey

Every loaf takes 36 hours. Every second is earned through patience and care.

1

Milling the Vision

We source whole, heritage grains from local mills that still use massive stone wheels. This cold-milling process preserves the oils, nutrients, and flavors often lost in industrial production.

2

Mixing with Intuition

No machines here. We mix our dough by hand, feeling the strength and elastic development through touch. It's a dialogue between the flour, the water, and the baker's hands.

3

Deep Fermentation

Our loaves rest in a cold environment for 24-36 hours. This slow pace allows the wild yeast (Maurice) to break down the gluten, enhancing flavor and making the bread easier to digest.

4

The Wood-Fired Dawn

In the pre-dawn hours, we score the loaves and slide them into our wood-fired oven. The intense heat creates that signature "shattering" crust and a deep, complex golden hue.

Meet Maurice

Maurice is our 4-year-old sourdough mother. She's a complex, living ecosystem of wild yeast and beneficial bacteria, fed daily with the finest stone-milled rye flour and Paris spring water.

Every single loaf from Aurore Sourdough—from our crusty country boules to our delicate pastries—is leavened exclusively by Maurice. No chemicals. No additives. Just pure, wild life.
